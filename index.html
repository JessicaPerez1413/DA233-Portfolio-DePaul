Overall Reflection

I have changed my perspective based on the topics of management information systems as I reached the end of the quarter. I found it interesting how much information systems are needed by a business to make it successful. However, we also learned about downfalls based on the case studies that happened and how we should do better. For example, I learned the principal driver for failure of information systems projects is not the technology itself but having proper planning, training, and aligning the goals of IT with the goals of the business.

My ability to analyze business processes and identify risks prior to technology implementation is my area of strength. The redesign of how Arctic wins receive goods allowed me the opportunity to apply the knowledge I had learned in class and understand how informal processes such as undocumented locations create substantial issues within an ERP system. Additionally, I am comfortable with the strategic aspect of a business's digital transformation by recommending the use of integrated cloud-based solutions such as SaaS products for small businesses. The assignment regarding Kirby's Fairway Golf Club gave me the ability to synthesize a number of concepts — from e-commerce, cloud computing, and CRM — into a single, cohesive strategic recommendation. I am much more comfortable explaining to others why IT and business alignment are important.

My goal is to strengthen my technical abilities concerning both database query creation and SQL syntax to build and run queries using Microsoft Access. While I can effectively describe how to create a database and what should result from a properly constructed one, creating an efficient and complete database from scratch is still difficult for me at this time. I would also like to apply Entity Relationship Diagrams more confidently, especially when many-to-many relationships exist and when lookup tables are needed. I want to expand my understanding of how all three types of information systems relate to each other — the transaction processing system, the functional area system, and the executive support system — as they are more interconnected within larger companies.

Overall, I am thankful for the broader view of business and management information systems that this class has provided.
